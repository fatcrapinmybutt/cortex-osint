## I. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This memorandum compiles Michigan damages law research across twenty (20) recovery theories
applicable to the Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC matter. Each theory is analyzed for legal authority,
damages formula, statutory floors/ceilings, multipliers, and strategic notes.

**Key Rule: MCL 600.1483 damages caps apply ONLY to medical malpractice — NOT to wrongful
eviction, MCPA violations, fraud, or any housing/property tort claim in Lane B.**

**Key Rule: MCL 600.6304 (several-only liability) — each defendant in a multi-defendant case
pays only their proportionate share of fault. Joint-and-several liability is abolished for
most Michigan torts. Exception: civil conspiracy makes all conspirators jointly liable.**

## III. STRATEGIC APPLICATION — SHADY OAKS PARK MHP LLC

### Factual Predicate Summary (Lane B)
Based on available intelligence, the following specific wrongful acts by Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC
and its principals/agents are alleged:

1. **Sewage/habitability failure** — Chronic sewage backup/leak causing uninhabitable conditions
2. **Utility interference** — Shutoff or interference with essential utility services
3. **Wrongful lockout/eviction** — Self-help eviction without lawful court process
4. **Property conversion** — Conversion of tenant's personal property
5. **Fraudulent lease practices** — Misrepresentations in lease terms, unauthorized fees, deceptive
   practices in connection with tenancy
6. **Retaliation** — Adverse action in response to tenant complaints/asserting rights

---

### Top-Tier Recovery Theories (Strongest Claims)

#### Tier 1 — High Certainty, High Value

**A. MCL 600.2918 (Wrongful Eviction) — ANCHOR CLAIM**
- If physical lockout without court order occurred: **3× actual damages OR $200 per occurrence**
  (whichever greater), mandatory attorney fees
- Each separate unlawful interference = separate "occurrence"
- Estimated: If monthly rent was $600 and lockout lasted 6 months, actual damages = $3,600;
  trebled = $10,800. Add $200 × N occurrences as alternative floor.
- **Action required**: Document each specific date/act of interference as a separate "occurrence"

**B. MCPA MCL 445.903/445.911 — STATUTORY FLOOR + FEES**
- Deceptive practices in connection with the rental/services = MCPA violation
- $250 minimum per transaction OR actual damages; willful = up to 3×
- **MANDATORY attorney fees** — even on small actual damages, fees may exceed judgment
- **Action required**: Identify specific MCPA subsection violated (e.g., §3(s) — unfair/deceptive;
  §3(l) — $5,000 floor if applicable)
- *Note*: Verify Shady Oaks is not exempt under *Smith v Globe Life* regulated industry exception

**C. MCL 600.2919a (Conversion) — TREBLE + MANDATORY FEES**
- If MHP converted tenant's personal property (retained belongings after lockout, damaged/destroyed
  property): up to 3× value of converted property + mandatory attorney fees
- *Aroma Wines*: "to their own use" broadly interpreted; refusal to return after demand = conversion
- **Action required**: Inventory all converted property with fair market values; send written demand
  for return; document refusal

**D. MCL 125.2324/125.2327 (MHP Commission Act) — MHP-SPECIFIC VIOLATIONS**
- Most directly applicable statute for MHP-specific misconduct (service cutoffs, retaliation,
  improper rent methods, fraudulent practices by operators)
- Actual damages + injunctive relief + attorney fees
- **Action required**: File LARA complaint concurrently with litigation (creates regulatory record)
- *Note*: Specific per-violation floors unresolved — needs supplemental research

---

#### Tier 2 — High Value, Moderate Evidentiary Burden

**E. MCL 554.139 (Habitability) — CHRONIC SEWAGE/UNSAFE CONDITIONS**
- Chronic sewage leak = breach of implied warranty of fitness and reasonable repair
- Contract damages: difference in rental value (uninhabitable site) — if fair rental value was
  $600/month and uninhabitable, recovery per month = full rent withheld
- No pre-suit notice required; no multiplier but serves as factual foundation for other claims
- Combines with MCL 125.2324 for cumulative statutory violation

**F. Fraud/Silent Fraud — LEASE MISREPRESENTATIONS**
- If lease misrepresented condition of park, utilities, or tenant rights: benefit-of-bargain damages
- Silent fraud: if known sewage/safety defects not disclosed at lease signing = actionable
- Exemplary damages available for willful/malicious conduct (compensating humiliation and outrage)
- Must plead with particularity; identify specific false statements and dates

**G. Civil Conspiracy (Admiral Ins Co v Columbia Cas Ins Co, 194 Mich App 300)**
- If multiple LLC members / management company / agents acted in concert
- Breaks intra-corporate immunity — name individuals acting OUTSIDE scope of employment
- Converts several-only liability into JOINT AND SEVERAL across all named defendants
- **Critical strategic use**: Ensure judgment collectability against individual members

---

#### Tier 3 — Supplemental / Conditional

**H. RICO (18 USC 1962) — IF SYSTEMATIC PATTERN**
- Mandatory 3× federal treble + mandatory attorney fees
- Requires pattern: 2+ mail fraud, wire fraud, or extortion predicate acts
- Strongest if: (1) fraudulent billing notices sent by mail, (2) same scheme used against multiple
  tenants, (3) ongoing threat of future conduct
- **Predicate acts to document**: Every fraudulent fee notice or threatening communication by mail
  or wire; extortionate demands for payment to avoid eviction
- NOTE: Federal venue (WDMI); strict pattern/causation scrutiny applies

**I. §1983 (42 USC §1983) — IF STATE ACTOR NEXUS**
- Applicability CONDITIONAL on state action nexus (Shady Oaks = private actor)
- State nexus theories: (a) municipal utility complicity in shutoff, (b) selective code enforcement
  non-response, (c) city/county participated in or endorsed the deprivation
- If nexus established: compensatory + punitive against individual officials + mandatory §1988 fees
- **Action required**: Identify any government actor who aided, directed, or ignored the deprivation

**J. Corporate Veil Piercing (Florence Cement Co. v. Vettraino, 292 Mich App 461)**
- If Shady Oaks LLC is undercapitalized, has commingled funds, or lacks corporate formalities
- Equitable remedy — extends ALL underlying tort damages to individual LLC members personally
- Critical for collectability if LLC lacks sufficient assets to satisfy judgment
- **Action required**: Obtain LLC formation documents, bank records, any evidence of fund commingling
