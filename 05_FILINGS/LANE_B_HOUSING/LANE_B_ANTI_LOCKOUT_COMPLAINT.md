## INTRODUCTION

This action arises from a deliberate, coordinated scheme by a multi-state manufactured housing cartel to illegally lock out Plaintiff from his lawfully-owned manufactured home at 1977 Whitehall Road, Lot 17, North Muskegon, Michigan 49445 ("the Property"). On July 17, 2025, at approximately mid-day, Deputy Douglas Schmidt of the Muskegon County Sheriff's Office, acting at the direction of Defendants Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC and HOA MI Retail Homes LLC, physically drilled the locks off of Plaintiff's manufactured home, which Plaintiff owned outright, and denied Plaintiff entry to his residence. This lockout was captured on Plaintiff's security cameras and was accomplished through fraudulent misrepresentations to the 60th District Court—including the material falsehood that Defendants owned the manufactured home—by a dissolved Michigan limited liability company whose corporate existence had terminated as of May 22, 2022.

Plaintiff brings this action under MCL 600.2918 (Anti-Lockout Statute), MCL 600.2919a (Conversion), MCL 600.2907 (Wrongful Use of Civil Proceedings/Fraud on Court), 42 USC § 1983 (Constitutional Deprivation of Property Without Due Process), and common-law trespass to chattels, seeking two times actual damages, statutory damages of $200 per day from July 17, 2025 through judgment, attorney fees, injunctive relief requiring return of title and possession, and punitive damages.

## GENERAL FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Plaintiff's Ownership of the Manufactured Home

11. Plaintiff is and at all relevant times has been the lawful owner of the manufactured home situated at 1977 Whitehall Road, Lot 17, North Muskegon, Michigan 49445 ("the Home"). Plaintiff holds legal title to the Home.

12. Plaintiff's tenancy at Lot 17 at the Shady Oaks Manufactured Housing Park was governed by the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act, MCL 125.2301 et seq., and the Michigan Manufactured Home Landlord-Tenant Act. As a homeowner within a manufactured housing community, Plaintiff is entitled to the full statutory protections applicable to manufactured home owners.

### B. The Dissolved Michigan Entity and Void Eviction Proceedings

13. "Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC" as a Michigan limited liability company was dissolved by the State of Michigan on **May 22, 2022**, as documented in records maintained by the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs ("LARA"), License Number 1201891.

14. Despite this dissolution, Defendants presented the dissolved Michigan entity to the 60th District Court in 2025 as the legal operator of the park and as the party with standing to pursue eviction proceedings against Plaintiff.

15. A dissolved Michigan LLC has no legal standing to sue, obtain court orders, or enforce writs of possession. MCL 450.4802 and MCL 450.4804 provide that a dissolved LLC retains only limited authority to wind up its affairs; a dissolved LLC may not initiate new litigation or obtain new court remedies to oust a tenant.

16. Any court order obtained by a dissolved and legally-nonexistent entity is void ab initio and provides no lawful basis for a writ of eviction or lot return.

17. Upon information and belief, Defendants knew at all times that Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC (MI) was dissolved, and deliberately concealed this material fact from the 60th District Court, constituting fraud upon that court.

### C. Defendants' Attempts to Coerce Plaintiff into Surrendering the Home

18. Prior to the eviction hearing that ultimately led to dismissal of Case 2025-002760-CZ, Defendants, through their authorized agents and employees, sent Plaintiff written communications—including emails—attempting to coerce Plaintiff into (a) selling his manufactured home to Defendants, and (b) "handing over the keys" to the Property.

19. At the time these coercive communications were transmitted, Defendants' own representatives expressed uncertainty in writing as to whether Defendants actually owned Plaintiff's manufactured home, demonstrating that Defendants knew they had no lawful right to possession of the Home.

20. These communications constitute attempted extortion and coercion in violation of Michigan law and federal law.

### D. The July 17, 2025 Unlawful Lockout

21. On **July 17, 2025**, at the direction of Defendants Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC (NJ) and HOA MI Retail Homes LLC, Defendant Deputy Douglas Schmidt and/or other Muskegon County Sheriff's Office personnel arrived at Plaintiff's Home at 1977 Whitehall Road, Lot 17.

22. Plaintiff was present on the premises and had active security cameras recording the exterior of the Home. Plaintiff personally called the Muskegon County Sheriff's Office on July 17, 2025, in response to observing on his security cameras that Defendants' agents were drilling the locks off the door of his Home.

23. Defendants caused the locks on Plaintiff's manufactured home to be physically drilled off and replaced, without lawful authority and without any valid court order against the owner of the manufactured home, thereby excluding Plaintiff from his own property.

24. The writ of eviction and lot return pursuant to which Deputy Schmidt purportedly acted was obtained by fraud and is void because: (a) it was obtained by a dissolved Michigan LLC with no legal existence or standing; (b) Defendants represented to the court that they owned Plaintiff's manufactured home when their own communications demonstrate uncertainty about that ownership; and (c) no valid court order for possession of Plaintiff's manufactured home—as distinguished from lot return—was ever obtained.

25. The lockout of Plaintiff from his owned manufactured home is a per se violation of MCL 600.2918.

### E. The Emergency Petition to 14th Circuit — Denial and Vacation

26. Following the unlawful lockout, Plaintiff timely petitioned the 14th Circuit Court for an emergency order to halt the eviction and restore possession, presenting evidence of the dissolved corporate standing, the coercive emails, and the security camera footage.

27. 14th Circuit Court Chief Judge **Kenneth Hoopes** denied Plaintiff's emergency petition and subsequently departed on vacation, leaving Plaintiff without any judicial remedy while Defendants maintained unlawful possession of the Home.

28. Chief Judge Hoopes is the husband of 60th District Court Judge **Maria Ladas-Hoopes**, who presided over the eviction proceedings. ## COUNT I — UNLAWFUL LOCKOUT (MCL 600.2918)

36. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

37. MCL 600.2918(1) provides that any person who is unlawfully locked out of a dwelling, or has personal property wrongfully taken or converted, may recover possession of the property, twice the actual damages, and reasonable attorney fees.

38. MCL 600.2918(2) provides that it is unlawful for a landlord or any person acting on behalf of a landlord to lock a tenant or a manufactured home owner out of a dwelling without a lawful court order.

39. Defendants Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC (NJ), HOA MI Retail Homes LLC, and Deputy Douglas Schmidt, acting in concert, caused Plaintiff to be locked out of his manufactured home on July 17, 2025, without any lawful court order (the writ was void), and without Plaintiff's consent.

40. As a direct and proximate result of this unlawful lockout, Plaintiff suffered actual damages including, but not limited to: loss of the use and enjoyment of his home; loss of personal property within the home; costs of temporary shelter; emotional distress; loss of employment; and diminishment of Plaintiff's health and welfare.

41. Plaintiff is entitled to:
    - Possession of the Home and all personal property therein;
    - **Two times actual damages** under MCL 600.2918(1);
    - **$200 per day** from July 17, 2025 through the date of judgment under MCL 600.2918(2) (alternative statutory measure);
    - Reasonable attorney fees;
    - All costs of this proceeding.

## COUNT II — CONVERSION (MCL 600.2919a)

42. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

43. By drilling Plaintiff's locks and seizing his manufactured home—his personal property under Michigan law—Defendants committed conversion.

44. MCL 600.2919a provides for 3× the value of the converted property, plus costs and attorney fees, where the conversion is accomplished by fraud, duress, or intentional interference.

45. Plaintiff is entitled to three times the value of the Home plus all personal property wrongfully seized, plus attorney fees and costs.
